---
title: "Root system"
description: "A finite set of vectors closed under reflections and satisfying integrality; the combinatorial data behind semisimple Lie theory."
---

A **(reduced) root system** is a finite subset $\Phi\subset V\setminus\{0\}$ of a real finite-dimensional inner product space $(V,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle)$ such that:

1. $\Phi$ spans $V$.
2. If $\alpha\in\Phi$, then the only scalar multiples of $\alpha$ in $\Phi$ are $\pm\alpha$.
3. For each $\alpha\in\Phi$, the reflection
   \[
   s_\alpha(v)=v-\frac{2\langle v,\alpha\rangle}{\langle \alpha,\alpha\rangle}\,\alpha
   \]
   preserves $\Phi$, i.e. $s_\alpha(\Phi)=\Phi$.
4. (**Integrality**) For all $\alpha,\beta\in\Phi$,
   \[
   \frac{2\langle \beta,\alpha\rangle}{\langle \alpha,\alpha\rangle}\in\mathbb Z.
   \]

The subgroup of $\mathrm{GL}(V)$ generated by the reflections $\{s_\alpha\}$ is the {{< knowl id="weyl-group" text="Weyl group" >}} of $\Phi$.

Root systems arise from semisimple Lie algebras: if $\mathfrak g$ is semisimple and $\mathfrak h$ is a {{< knowl id="cartan-subalgebra" text="Cartan subalgebra" >}}, then the set of roots $\Phi\subset \mathfrak h^*$ (see {{< knowl id="root-lie-algebra" text="root of a Lie algebra" >}}) becomes a root system after identifying $\mathfrak h^*$ with a real inner product space via the {{< knowl id="killing-form" text="Killing form" >}}. The corresponding {{< knowl id="root-space-decomposition" text="root space decomposition" >}} is the bridge between algebra and combinatorics.

Choosing a {{< knowl id="positive-root" text="positive system" >}} picks out a basis of {{< knowl id="simple-root" text="simple roots" >}}, from which one constructs the {{< knowl id="cartan-matrix" text="Cartan matrix" >}} and {{< knowl id="dynkin-diagram" text="Dynkin diagram" >}}. This data underlies the {{< knowl id="classification-simple-lie-algebras" text="classification of simple Lie algebras" >}}.
