For a {{< knowl id="set" text="set" >}} $A$, the **identity function** on $A$ is the {{< knowl id="function" text="function" >}}
$$
\mathrm{id}_A\colon A\to A,\qquad \mathrm{id}_A(a)=a.
$$

The identity function is {{< knowl id="bijective-function" text="bijective" >}}. Identity functions are characterized by their behavior under {{< knowl id="composition" text="composition" >}}: for any $f\colon A\to B$, one has $\mathrm{id}_B\circ f=f$ and $f\circ \mathrm{id}_A=f$.

**Examples:**
- On $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}}(x)=x$.
- On a finite set $A=\{1,2,3\}$, $\mathrm{id}_A$ fixes each element.
