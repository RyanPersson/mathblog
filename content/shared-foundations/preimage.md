Let $f\colon A\to B$ be a {{< knowl id="function" text="function" >}} and let $T\subseteq B$ be a {{< knowl id="subset" text="subset" >}}. The **preimage** (or inverse image) of $T$ under $f$ is
$$
f^{-1}(T)=\{a\in A: f(a)\in T\}\subseteq A.
$$

Preimages are defined for arbitrary functions (not only {{< knowl id="bijective-function" text="bijections" >}}) and are fundamental in pulling back structure along maps.

**Examples:**
- If $f(x)=x^2$ on $\mathbb{R}$ and $T=[0,1]$, then $f^{-1}(T)=[-1,1]$.
- If $f\colon\mathbb{Z}\to\mathbb{Z}$ is $f(n)=2n$ and $T=\{0\}$, then $f^{-1}(T)=\{0\}$.
