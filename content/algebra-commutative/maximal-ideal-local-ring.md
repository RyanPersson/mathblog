---
title: "Maximal ideal of a local ring"
description: "In a local ring (R,m), there is a unique maximal ideal m, and the units are exactly R \\ m."
---

Let $R$ be a {{< knowl id="local-ring" text="local ring" >}}. Its defining feature is that it has a **unique** {{< knowl id="maximal-ideal" section="algebra-rings" text="maximal ideal" >}}, usually denoted $\mathfrak m$.

## Statement

If $(R,\mathfrak m)$ is a local ring, then:

1. $\mathfrak m$ is the unique maximal ideal of $R$.
2. An element $u\in R$ is a {{< knowl id="unit" section="algebra-rings" text="unit" >}} **iff** $u\notin \mathfrak m$. Equivalently,
   \[
   R^\times = R\setminus \mathfrak m.
   \]
3. The {{< knowl id="jacobson-radical" section="algebra-rings" text="Jacobson radical" >}} of $R$ equals $\mathfrak m$:
   \[
   J(R)=\mathfrak m.
   \]
4. Via the {{< knowl id="residue-field" text="residue field" >}} $k=R/\mathfrak m$, an element $u\in R$ is a unit **iff** its image in $k$ is nonzero.

## Examples

1. **$\mathbb Z_{(p)}$.**  
   In the {{< knowl id="localization-at-prime" text="local ring" >}} $\mathbb Z_{(p)}$, the maximal ideal is $p\mathbb Z_{(p)}$. The units are fractions $a/b$ with $p\nmid a$.

2. **$k[[x]]$.**  
   In {{< knowl id="formal-power-series-ring" section="algebra-rings" text="k[[x]]" >}}, the maximal ideal is $(x)$. A power series is a unit iff its constant term is nonzero.

3. **$k[x,y]_{(x,y)}$.**  
   The localization of $k[x,y]$ at the maximal ideal $(x,y)$ is local with maximal ideal generated by $x$ and $y$.
