---
title: "Codimension-One Subspaces Give Direct Sum Decompositions"
description: "If codim(L)=1 and x0∉L, then X=L⊕span{x0}."
---

Let $X$ be a {{< knowl id="vector-space" section="shared-linear-algebra" text="vector space" >}} and let $L\subset X$ be a {{< knowl id="linear-subspace" text="subspace" >}} with {{< knowl id="codimension" text="codim(L)" >}} $=1$. If $x_0\notin L$, then:

**Proposition**:
$$
X = L \oplus \operatorname{span}\{x_0\},
$$
where $\oplus$ denotes the {{< knowl id="direct-sum-of-subspaces" text="direct sum" >}}.

**Context:**
This shows that a codimension-one subspace is "one linear dimension short" of the whole space. It is the structural fact behind representing hyperplanes as kernels (or level sets) of nonzero linear functionals.
