Let $X$ be a {{< knowl id="vector-space" section="shared-linear-algebra" text="vector space" >}} over $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$, and let $f:X\to\mathbb{K}$ be a nonzero linear functional with {{< knowl id="image-and-kernel-linear-isomorphism" text="kernel" >}} $\ker f$.

**Proposition**:
$$
\operatorname{codim}(\ker f)=1.
$$

**Context:**
The kernel of a nonzero functional is a codimension-one subspace, and translates of such kernels are precisely {{< knowl id="hyperplane" text="hyperplanes" >}} in real vector spaces; see {{< knowl id="hyperplanes-are-level-sets-of-nonzero-linear-functionals" text="hyperplanes as level sets" >}}.
