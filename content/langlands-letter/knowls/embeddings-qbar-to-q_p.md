---
title: "Choosing Embeddings $\\overline{\\mathbb{Q}}\\hookrightarrow \\overline{\\mathbb{Q}}_p$"
description: "How a choice of $p$-adic embedding fixes a decomposition group and conjugates Frobenius/Satake data"
---

Choosing an embedding $\iota_p:\overline{\mathbb{Q}}\hookrightarrow \overline{\mathbb{Q}}_p$ is equivalent to choosing a prime of $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ above $p$.

This identifies a **decomposition group** in $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ with the local Galois group $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_p/\mathbb{Q}_p)$, and hence fixes a notion of {{< knowl id="frobenius-unramified" section="langlands-letter/knowls" text="Frobenius" >}} at $p$.

Changing $\iota_p$ conjugates the resulting Frobenius and therefore conjugates the Satake parameter $\alpha_p$ in the {{< knowl id="l-group-satake-parameter" section="langlands-letter/knowls" text="$L$-group" >}}.

**Consequence used in the letter:** the Euler product {{< knowl id="euler-product-and-local-factor" section="langlands-letter/knowls" text="$L(s)=\\prod_p L_p(s)$" >}} changes only by finitely many local factors (the "bad primes").
