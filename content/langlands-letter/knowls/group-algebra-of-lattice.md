---
title: "Group Algebra of a Lattice and Multiplicative Basis"
description: "The algebra $\\mathbb{C}[L]$ with basis elements $\\xi_\\lambda$ and $\\xi_\\lambda\\xi_\\mu=\\xi_{\\lambda+\\mu}$"
---

Let $L$ be a free abelian group (a **lattice**), e.g. $L=X^*(T)$ (see {{< knowl id="maximal-torus-weight-lattice" section="langlands-letter/knowls" text="$X^*(T)$" >}}).

The **group algebra** $\mathbb{C}[L]$ is the $\mathbb{C}$-vector space with basis $\{\xi_\lambda\}_{\lambda\in L}$ and multiplication
$
\xi_\lambda\cdot \xi_\mu=\xi_{\lambda+\mu}.
$

If $t\in T(\mathbb{C})$, then $\lambda(t)\in\mathbb{C}^\times$ and one "evaluates" by $\xi_\lambda(t):=\lambda(t)$.

**In the letter:** Satake identifies the spherical Hecke algebra with invariants in such a group algebra on a dual lattice.
