---
title: "Sum of ideals"
description: "The ideal consisting of all sums of an element from each of two ideals."
---

Given ideals $I,J$ in a {{</* knowl id="ring" text="ring" */>}} $R$, their **sum** is
\[
I+J=\{\,i+j : i\in I,\ j\in J\,\}.
\]
It is the smallest {{</* knowl id="ideal" text="ideal" */>}} of $R$ containing both $I$ and $J$.

The sum interacts with quotients via the {{</* knowl id="second-isomorphism-theorem-rings" text="second isomorphism theorem" */>}} and measures “comaximality” when $I+J=R$. In $\mathbb Z$, sums of ideals encode the {{</* knowl id="gcd" text="gcd" */>}}.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb Z$, $(m)+(n)=(\gcd(m,n))$.
- In $k[x,y]$, $(x)+(y)=(x,y)$.
- If $I\subseteq J$, then $I+J=J$.
