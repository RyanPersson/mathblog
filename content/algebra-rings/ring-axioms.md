---
title: "Ring axioms"
description: "Axioms defining a ring as an abelian group under addition with associative multiplication distributing over addition."
---

The **ring axioms** specify a set \(R\) equipped with two {{</* knowl id="binary-operation" text="binary operations" */>}} \(+\) and \(\cdot\) such that:

1. \((R,+)\) is an {{</* knowl id="abelian-group" text="abelian group" */>}} (with identity element \(0\)).
2. Multiplication is associative: \((ab)c=a(bc)\) for all \(a,b,c\in R\).
3. Distributive laws hold: \(a(b+c)=ab+ac\) and \((a+b)c=ac+bc\) for all \(a,b,c\in R\).

These axioms define a {{</* knowl id="ring" text="ring" */>}} (not necessarily {{</* knowl id="unital-ring" text="unital" */>}}, and not necessarily {{</* knowl id="commutative-ring" text="commutative" */>}}). Most structural notions—such as an {{</* knowl id="ideal" text="ideal" */>}} and a {{</* knowl id="ring-homomorphism" text="ring homomorphism" */>}}—are formulated relative to this axiomatic package.
