---
title: "Cancellation in integral domains"
description: "In an integral domain, nonzero elements satisfy left and right cancellation."
---

**Cancellation in integral domains**: Let $D$ be an integral domain and let $a,b,c\in D$ with $a\neq 0$. If $ab=ac$, then $b=c$. Likewise, if $ba=ca$, then $b=c$.

In an {{</* knowl id="integral-domain" text="integral domain" */>}}, every nonzero element is a {{</* knowl id="regular-element" text="regular element" */>}} (equivalently, there are no nonzero {{</* knowl id="zero-divisor" text="zero divisors" */>}}), and this is exactly what makes cancellation possible. The same injectivity argument is the starting point for constructing the {{</* knowl id="fraction-field" text="field of fractions" */>}}.
