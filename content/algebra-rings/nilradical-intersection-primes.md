---
title: "Nilradical equals intersection of prime ideals"
description: "In a commutative ring, the nilradical is the intersection of all prime ideals."
---

**Nilradical equals intersection of prime ideals**: Let $R$ be a commutative ring. Then
\[
\mathrm{Nil}(R)=\bigcap_{\mathfrak p\in \mathrm{Spec}(R)} \mathfrak p,
\]
i.e., an element is nilpotent if and only if it lies in every prime ideal. Equivalently, $\mathrm{Nil}(R)=\sqrt{(0)}$.

Equivalently, it is the {{</* knowl id="radical-of-ideal" text="radical" */>}} of $(0)$, so $R/\mathrm{Nil}(R)$ is {{</* knowl id="reduced-ring" text="reduced" */>}}.
