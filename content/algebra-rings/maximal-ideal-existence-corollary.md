**Every nontrivial commutative ring has a maximal ideal**: If $R$ is a commutative ring with $1$ and $1\neq 0$, then there exists a maximal ideal $\mathfrak m\lhd R$.

This follows from {{</* knowl id="existence-of-maximal-ideals" text="the existence theorem for maximal ideals" */>}}, whose proof applies {{</* knowl id="zorns-lemma" text="Zorn's Lemma" */>}} to the set of proper {{</* knowl id="ideal" text="ideals" */>}} of a {{</* knowl id="commutative-ring" text="commutative ring" */>}} with $1$ to produce a {{</* knowl id="maximal-ideal" text="maximal ideal" */>}}.
