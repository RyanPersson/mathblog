---
title: "Connection 1-form on a principal bundle"
description: "Definition of a principal connection 1-form and the horizontal distribution it determines."
---

Let $\pi:P\to M$ be a {{< knowl id="principal-g-bundle" text="principal G-bundle" >}}}} (with the {{< knowl id="convention-principal-bundles-use-a-right-g-action-on-p" text="standard right action convention" >}}}}), and let $\mathfrak g$ be the {{< knowl id="lie-algebra-of-a-lie-group" text="Lie algebra" >}}}} of $G$.

## Definition
A **(principal) connection 1-form** on $P$ is a $\mathfrak g$-valued 1-form
\[
\omega \in \Omega^1(P;\mathfrak g)
\]
such that:

1. **Reproduction on verticals.** For every $X\in\mathfrak g$, if $X^\#$ denotes the {{< knowl id="convention-fundamental-vector-field-x-is-defined-using-the-right-action" text="fundamental vector field" >}}}} on $P$ generated by $X$ (so $X^\#$ is vertical), then
   \[
   \omega(X^\#)=X .
   \]
   (This is the {{< knowl id="reproduction-property-x" text="reproduction property" >}}}}.)

2. **Equivariance.** For every $g\in G$,
   \[
   (R_g)^*\omega = \mathrm{Ad}_{g^{-1}}\omega,
   \]
   where $\mathrm{Ad}$ is the {{< knowl id="adjoint-action-of-a-lie-group" text="adjoint action" >}}}} of $G$ on $\mathfrak g$.

These two axioms are equivalent to specifying a {{< knowl id="principal-connection" text="principal connection" >}}}}: the horizontal space at $p\in P$ is
\[
H_p := \ker(\omega_p)\subset T_pP,
\]
giving a smooth $G$-invariant {{< knowl id="horizontal-distribution" text="horizontal distribution" >}}}} complementary to the {{< knowl id="vertical-subbundle" text="vertical subbundle" >}}}}.

### Local form
If $s:U\to P$ is a smooth local section (as in {{< knowl id="construction-local-trivialization-from-a-local-section" text="constructing a local trivialization from a local section" >}}}}), then the pullback
\[
A := s^*\omega \in \Omega^1(U;\mathfrak g)
\]
is the corresponding {{< knowl id="local-connection-1-form" text="local connection 1-form" >}}}} (“gauge potential”) on $U$.

### Curvature
From $\omega$ one defines the {{< knowl id="curvature-2-form-of-a-principal-connection" text="curvature 2-form" >}}}}
\[
\Omega := d\omega + \tfrac12[\omega\wedge \omega] \in \Omega^2(P;\mathfrak g),
\]
which is horizontal and equivariant, hence descends to local curvature forms on $M$.

## Examples
1. **The bundle $G\to \mathrm{pt}$.** View $P=G$ as a principal $G$-bundle over a point with right multiplication. The {{< knowl id="left-maurercartan-form" text="left Maurer–Cartan form" >}}}} $\theta_L$ is a connection 1-form: it reproduces generators of the right action and satisfies the required equivariance under right translation.

2. **Trivial bundle with a chosen potential.** For $P=M\times G$, choose any $A\in\Omega^1(M;\mathfrak g)$. Writing $(x,g)\in M\times G$, define
   \[
   \omega := \mathrm{Ad}_{g^{-1}}(\pi_M^*A) + \theta_L ,
   \]
   where $\theta_L$ is pulled back from the $G$-factor and $\pi_M:P\to M$ is projection. With respect to the global section $s(x)=(x,e)$, the local form is exactly $s^*\omega=A$. When $A=0$ this gives the {{< knowl id="flat-connection-a0-on-a-trivial-bundle" text="standard flat connection" >}}}}.

3. **Hopf fibration (abelian case).** On the {{< knowl id="hopf-fibration-s3s2-as-a-principal-u-bundle" text="Hopf bundle" >}}}} $S^3\to S^2$ with structure group $U(1)$, there is a canonical connection 1-form (the {{< knowl id="dirac-monopole-connection-on-the-hopf-bundle" text="Dirac monopole connection" >}}}}). Since $U(1)$ is abelian, the equivariance condition simplifies, and one can write an explicit $\mathfrak u(1)$-valued 1-form on $S^3\subset\mathbb C^2$ that annihilates horizontals and evaluates to the generator on the circle fibers.
