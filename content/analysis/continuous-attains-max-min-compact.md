---
title: "Continuous function attains max and min on a compact set"
description: "On compact domains, continuous functions achieve their extrema"
---

**Corollary (Extreme Value Theorem)**: Let $(X,d)$ be a {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}} {{< knowl id="metric-space" text="metric space" >}} and let $f:X\to\mathbb{R}$ be {{< knowl id="continuity-on-a-set" text="continuous" >}}. Then there exist $x_{\min},x_{\max}\in X$ such that
$
f(x_{\min})={{< knowl id="minimum" text="min" >}}_{x\in X} f(x),\qquad f(x_{\max})={{< knowl id="maximum" text="max" >}}_{x\in X} f(x).
$

**Connection to parent theorem**:
This is the {{< knowl id="extreme-value-theorem" text="extreme value theorem" >}}; it follows because $f(X)$ is compact in $\mathbb{R}$, hence has a minimum and maximum.
