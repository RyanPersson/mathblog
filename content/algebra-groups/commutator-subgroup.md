---
title: "Commutator subgroup"
description: "The subgroup generated by all commutators in a group"
---

Let $G$ be a {{</* knowl id="group" text="group" */>}}. The **commutator subgroup** (or **derived subgroup**) of $G$ is
$
[G,G]=\langle [g,h] : g,h\in G\rangle,
$
the {{</* knowl id="generated-subgroup" text="subgroup generated" */>}} by all {{</* knowl id="commutator" text="commutators" */>}} of elements of $G$.

The commutator subgroup is a {{</* knowl id="normal-subgroup" text="normal subgroup" */>}} of $G$. The quotient {{</* knowl id="quotient-group" text="quotient group" */>}} $G/[G,G]$ is abelian, and $[G,G]$ is the smallest normal subgroup $N\lhd G$ for which $G/N$ is abelian (equivalently: $G/[G,G]$ is the "largest" abelian quotient of $G$). Iterating commutator subgroups yields the {{</* knowl id="derived-series" text="derived series" */>}}, central to solvability.

**Examples:**
- If $G$ is abelian, then $[G,G]=\{e\}$.
- In $S_3$, one has $[S_3,S_3]=A_3$ (the alternating subgroup of order $3$).
- In the dihedral group $D_8=\langle r,s\mid r^4=s^2=e,\ srs=r^{-1}\rangle$, the commutator subgroup is $\{e,r^2\}$.
