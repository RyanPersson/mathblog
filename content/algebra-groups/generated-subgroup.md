---
title: "Generated Subgroup"
description: "The smallest subgroup containing a given subset"
---

Let $G$ be a {{< knowl id="group" text="group" >}} and let $S\subseteq G$ be a {{< knowl id="subset" section="shared-foundations" text="subset" >}}. The **subgroup generated by $S$**, denoted $\langle S\rangle$, is defined by
$
\langle S\rangle \;=\; \bigcap\{\,H\le G : S\subseteq H\,\},
$
the {{< knowl id="intersection" section="shared-foundations" text="intersection" >}} of all {{< knowl id="subgroup" text="subgroups" >}} of $G$ that contain $S$.

Equivalently, $\langle S\rangle$ is the set of all finite products of elements of $S$ and their inverses (i.e. all "words" in $S\cup S^{-1}$). When $S=\{g\}$ is a singleton, $\langle S\rangle$ is a {{< knowl id="cyclic-subgroup" text="cyclic subgroup" >}}.

**Examples:**
- In $(\mathbb{Z},+)$, $\langle 6,15\rangle = 3\mathbb{Z}$.
- In $S_3$, the set $\{(12),(123)\}$ generates all of $S_3$.
- In $\mathbb{R}^{\times}$, the subset $\{-1\}$ generates $\{1,-1\}$.
