---
title: "Galois group"
description: "The group of field automorphisms of an extension that fix the base field pointwise."
---

Let \(K/F\) be a {{< knowl id="field-extension" text="field extension" >}}.

**Definition (Galois group).** The *Galois group* of \(K/F\), denoted \(\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)\), is the set of all {{< knowl id="field-automorphism" text="field automorphisms" >}} \(\sigma:K\to K\) that fix \(F\) pointwise (i.e., \(\sigma(a)=a\) for all \(a\in F\)).
With composition as the operation, \(\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)\) is a group (a subgroup of the {{< knowl id="automorphism-group" section="algebra-groups" text="automorphism group" >}} of the field \(K\)).

When \(K/F\) is a finite {{< knowl id="galois-extension" text="Galois extension" >}}, one has \(|\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)|=[K:F]\), and the base field \(F\) is the {{< knowl id="fixed-field" text="fixed field" >}} of \(\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)\).

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt2)/\mathbb{Q})\) has two elements: the identity and the automorphism sending \(\sqrt2\mapsto -\sqrt2\). Thus it is cyclic of order \(2\).
2. For finite fields, \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p)\) is cyclic of order \(n\), generated by the Frobenius automorphism \(x\mapsto x^p\) (compare {{< knowl id="finite-field-galois-group-cyclic" text="finite-field Galois groups are cyclic" >}}).
3. For a {{< knowl id="cyclotomic-extension" text="cyclotomic extension" >}} \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q}\), every automorphism is determined by \(\zeta_n\mapsto \zeta_n^a\) with \(\gcd(a,n)=1\), giving an isomorphism
   \[
   \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q}) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times.
   \]
