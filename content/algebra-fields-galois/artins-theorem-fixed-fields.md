---
title: "Artin's theorem on fixed fields"
description: "A finite group of field automorphisms yields a finite Galois extension with degree equal to the group order."
---

Let \(L\) be a field and let \(G\) be a finite subgroup of the group of {{< knowl id="field-automorphism" text="field automorphisms" >}} of \(L\). Define the {{< knowl id="fixed-field" text="fixed field" >}}
\[
L^G=\{x\in L:\sigma(x)=x\ \text{for all}\ \sigma\in G\}.
\]

**Theorem (Artin).** If \(G\le \mathrm{Aut}(L)\) is finite and \(K=L^G\), then:
1. \(L/K\) is a finite {{< knowl id="galois-extension" text="Galois extension" >}};
2. the restriction map identifies \(G\) with the full {{< knowl id="galois-group" text="Galois group" >}}:
   \[
   \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)=G;
   \]
3. in particular,
   \[
   [L:K]=|G|.
   \]
This is a foundational input to the {{< knowl id="fundamental-theorem-galois-theory" text="fundamental theorem of Galois theory" >}} and explains why fixed fields and automorphism groups match so tightly.

### Examples
1. \(L=\mathbb{C}\), \(G=\{1,\mathrm{conj}\}\) where \(\mathrm{conj}(z)=\overline{z}\).  
   Then \(L^G=\mathbb{R}\), so \(\mathbb{C}/\mathbb{R}\) is Galois with \([\mathbb{C}:\mathbb{R}]=2=|G|\).

2. \(L=\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt2,\sqrt3)\), and let \(G\) be the subgroup of \(\mathrm{Aut}(L)\) generated by \(\sqrt2\mapsto-\sqrt2\) and \(\sqrt3\mapsto-\sqrt3\).  
   Then \(G\) has order \(4\), its fixed field is \(\mathbb{Q}\), and Artin’s theorem gives \([L:\mathbb{Q}]=4\).

3. \(L=\mathbb{F}_{p^n}\) and let \(G=\langle \varphi\rangle\) where \(\varphi(x)=x^p\) is Frobenius (see {{< knowl id="frobenius-endomorphism" text="Frobenius endomorphism" >}}).  
   Then \(|G|=n\), \(L^G=\mathbb{F}_p\), and Artin’s theorem recovers that \(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p\) is Galois of degree \(n\).
