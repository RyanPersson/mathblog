---
title: "Fundamental theorem of Galois theory"
description: "For a finite Galois extension L/K, intermediate fields correspond to subgroups of Gal(L/K)."
---

Let \(L/K\) be a finite {{< knowl id="galois-extension" text="Galois extension" >}}, and write
\[
G = \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)
\]
for its {{< knowl id="galois-group" text="Galois group" >}}.

For a subgroup \(H\le G\), the {{< knowl id="fixed-field" text="fixed field" >}} of \(H\) is
\[
L^H=\{x\in L:\sigma(x)=x\text{ for all }\sigma\in H\}.
\]
For an {{< knowl id="intermediate-field" text="intermediate field" >}} \(K\subseteq E\subseteq L\), write
\[
\mathrm{Gal}(L/E)=\{\sigma\in G:\sigma|_E=\mathrm{id}_E\}.
\]

**Theorem (Fundamental theorem of Galois theory).** The assignments
\[
H \longmapsto L^H
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
E \longmapsto \mathrm{Gal}(L/E)
\]
give an inclusion-reversing bijection between subgroups \(H\le G\) and intermediate fields \(E\) with \(K\subseteq E\subseteq L\). Under this correspondence:
1. \([L:E]=|\,\mathrm{Gal}(L/E)\,|\) and \([E:K]=[G:\mathrm{Gal}(L/E)]\).
2. \(E/K\) is Galois if and only if \(\mathrm{Gal}(L/E)\trianglelefteq G\) is a normal subgroup; in that case there is a natural isomorphism
   \[
   \mathrm{Gal}(E/K)\cong G/\mathrm{Gal}(L/E).
   \]

This is the conceptual content packaged in the explicit {{< knowl id="galois-correspondence" text="Galois correspondence" >}}.

### Examples
1. \(L=\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt2)\) over \(K=\mathbb{Q}\).  
   Then \(G\cong C_2\) has exactly two subgroups, so the only intermediate fields are \(\mathbb{Q}\) and \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt2)\).

2. \(L=\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_8)\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\), where \(\zeta_8\) is a primitive 8th root of unity (see {{< knowl id="cyclotomic-extension" text="cyclotomic extensions" >}}).  
   Here \(G\cong (\mathbb{Z}/8\mathbb{Z})^\times\cong C_2\times C_2\), which has three distinct index-2 subgroups. Correspondingly, \(L/\mathbb{Q}\) has three distinct quadratic intermediate fields.

3. Finite fields: \(L=\mathbb{F}_{p^n}\) over \(K=\mathbb{F}_p\).  
   The extension is Galois, and \(G\) is cyclic of order \(n\) generated by {{< knowl id="frobenius-endomorphism" text="Frobenius" >}}; see {{< knowl id="finite-field-galois-group-cyclic" text="the cyclic Galois group of finite fields" >}}.  
   Subgroups of a cyclic group correspond to divisors of \(n\), so the intermediate fields are exactly \(\mathbb{F}_{p^d}\) for \(d\mid n\).
